# Principles

- Prefer one typed organ with fixtures and validators over many descriptive leaves.
- Expose cluster, card, source, and receipt handles before source bodies.
- Keep standards broad enough to cover typed kinds; do not cap them for aesthetics.
- Make expected negative cases first-class evidence.
- Treat tool absence, missing organs, and private-boundary failures as typed states, not silent skips.
- Keep generated receipts machine-readable and small enough for cold agents.
- Preserve anti-claims beside every fixture-backed proof.
- Build the next organ only after the current root can say what is missing.
